# Daily AI & Tech Trends - 2026-04-13

## Pick Up

1. [Claude Mythos: Preparing for a World Where AI Finds and Exploits Vulnerabilities Faster Than Ever](https://www.wiz.io/blog/claude-mythos)
   - Why I recommend: Wiz's latest research paper published April 10 examines how AI agents are beginning to close the gap on human-speed vulnerability discovery and exploitation — directly relevant for ML engineers building or evaluating security-aware AI systems, and a must-read for understanding where autonomous offensive AI stands today.

2. [All elementary functions from a single binary operator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21852)
   - Why I recommend: This arXiv paper (the highest-scored HN story today at 764 points) demonstrates that all elementary mathematical functions can be derived from a single binary operator — a foundational theoretical result with implications for numerical libraries, hardware design, and symbolic ML systems.

3. [Someone Bought 30 WordPress Plugins and Planted a Backdoor in All of Them](https://anchor.host/someone-bought-30-wordpress-plugins-and-planted-a-backdoor-in-all-of-them/)
   - Why I recommend: A real-world supply chain attack targeting the plugin acquisition pipeline — a pattern that mirrors the TeamPCP LiteLLM/Trivy/axios campaigns tracked by Wiz; essential awareness for teams running ML workloads on shared infrastructure or open-source tooling.

4. [Servo is now available on crates.io](https://servo.org/blog/2026/04/13/servo-0.1.0-release/)
   - Why I recommend: The Servo browser engine reaching its first crates.io release (0.1.0) is a milestone for the Rust ecosystem — signals growing maturity of Rust for systems-level web infrastructure and has implications for embedding browser engines in AI-driven applications.

5. [Nothing Ever Happens: Polymarket bot that always buys No on non-sports markets](https://github.com/sterlingcrispin/nothing-ever-happens)
   - Why I recommend: A striking empirical observation that predicting "nothing will happen" outperforms more complex strategies on most Polymarket markets — a useful calibration data point about base rate reasoning and the limits of prediction models, relevant to any ML engineer working on probabilistic forecasting.

6. [The Future of Everything Is Lies, I Guess: Safety](https://aphyr.com/posts/417-the-future-of-everything-is-lies-i-guess-safety)
   - Why I recommend: Kyle Kingsbury (aphyr) continues his sharp-edged series on software honesty with a focus on safety claims — particularly relevant as AI safety marketing proliferates; provides a framework for evaluating whether safety assertions from vendors or papers are substantive or performative.

7. [This year's insane timeline of hacks](https://ringmast4r.substack.com/p/we-may-be-living-through-the-most)
   - Why I recommend: A curated timeline of major 2026 security incidents showing the accelerating pace and scale of attacks — valuable for situational awareness and understanding the threat environment in which ML-powered systems are being deployed.

8. [Bringing Security Visibility to Vercel with Wiz](https://www.wiz.io/blog/introducing-wiz-vercel-integration)
   - Why I recommend: As ML inference increasingly runs on serverless edge platforms like Vercel, having security visibility into those deployments becomes critical — this integration closes a meaningful gap for teams serving models via Next.js or edge functions.
