# Daily AI & Tech Trends - 2026-05-16

## Pick Up

1. [SANA-WM, a 2.6B open-source world model for 1-minute 720p video](https://nvlabs.github.io/Sana/WM/)
   - Why I recommend: A compact 2.6B-parameter open-source model generating coherent 1-minute 720p video is a significant leap for accessible video generation research — the parameter efficiency relative to output quality makes it immediately relevant for ML engineers studying generative architectures.

2. [Orthrus-Qwen3: up to 7.8×tokens/forward on Qwen3, identical output distribution](https://github.com/chiennv2000/orthrus)
   - Why I recommend: A 7.8× throughput improvement on Qwen3 with no change in output distribution is a striking inference optimization result; understanding how this is achieved (likely speculative decoding or architectural tricks) is directly applicable to production LLM serving.

3. [Δ-Mem: Efficient Online Memory for Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12357)
   - Why I recommend: Persistent, efficient online memory for LLMs is an open problem central to agentic systems — this arXiv paper landing on HN with 172 points and 45 comments signals it has a practically relevant approach worth reading for anyone building memory-augmented agents.

4. [DeepSeek-V4-Flash means LLM steering is interesting again](https://www.seangoedecke.com/steering-vectors/)
   - Why I recommend: Steering vectors (activating latent concepts via residual stream interventions) become practical at scale with fast inference models; this post explains how DeepSeek-V4-Flash changes the calculus and is a good primer on mechanistic interpretability for practitioners.

5. [Frontier AI has broken the open CTF format](https://kabir.au/blog/the-ctf-scene-is-dead)
   - Why I recommend: AI agents now solve CTF challenges that were designed for human teams, breaking the core assumption of open competitions — this is both a concrete benchmark of AI capability progress and an important signal for security engineers who design evaluations or train on CTF data.

6. [Fragnesia: Linux Kernel Local Privilege Escalation via ESP-in-TCP](https://www.wiz.io/blog/fragnesia-linux-kernel-local-privilege-escalation-via-esp-in-tcp)
   - Why I recommend: A fresh kernel LPE discovered by Wiz Research exploiting an unusual ESP-in-TCP path — the low-level Linux networking detail and the exploitation technique are worth understanding for anyone running or securing Linux infrastructure.

7. [Mini Shai-Hulud Strikes Again: TanStack + more npm Packages Compromised](https://www.wiz.io/blog/mini-shai-hulud-strikes-again-tanstack-more-npm-packages-compromised)
   - Why I recommend: A recurring supply chain threat actor has now compromised TanStack (a widely used frontend library), meaning projects depending on it may be silently executing malicious code — check your lock files and CI dependency pinning immediately.

8. [Red Agent and Claude Opus: Securing Production Targets at Scale](https://www.wiz.io/blog/red-agent-claude-opus)
   - Why I recommend: Wiz details how they deploy an AI red-team agent (Claude Opus) autonomously against production cloud environments — a concrete example of agentic AI in offensive security that raises important questions about authorization boundaries and detection for ML engineers building or operating AI systems.
