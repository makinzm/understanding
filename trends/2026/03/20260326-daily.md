4. [HyperAgents: Self-referential self-improving agents](https://github.com/facebookresearch/hyperagents)
   - Why I recommend: Facebook Research releases a framework for self-referential, self-improving agents — agents that can modify their own code and architecture. Highly relevant to understanding the frontier of autonomous AI system design.

5. [Introducing the Green Agent: AI-Powered Remediation for the Cloud](https://www.wiz.io/blog/introducing-wiz-green-agent)
   - Why I recommend: Wiz's new AI agent automatically remediates cloud security findings. A concrete example of agentic AI applied to security operations — useful for understanding how AI agents are being integrated into real-world cloud workflows.

6. [Introducing the Wiz Red Agent- AI-Powered Attacker](https://www.wiz.io/blog/introducing-the-wiz-red-agent)
   - Why I recommend: Wiz's AI-powered red team agent that simulates adversarial attack paths. Pairs with the Green Agent to create a complete AI-driven offense/defense security loop — a notable milestone in applying LLM agents to cybersecurity.

7. [From zero to a RAG system: successes and failures](https://en.andros.dev/blog/aa31d744/from-zero-to-a-rag-system-successes-and-failures/)
   - Why I recommend: An honest engineering retrospective on building a production RAG system, covering what worked and what failed. Practical lessons for ML engineers building retrieval-augmented applications, especially around chunking, retrieval quality, and latency.

8. [End of "Chat Control": EU parliament stops mass surveillance](https://www.patrick-breyer.de/en/end-of-chat-control-eu-parliament-stops-mass-surveillance-in-voting-thriller-paving-the-way-for-genuine-child-protection/)
   - Why I recommend: A significant EU policy victory blocking mandatory client-side scanning of encrypted messages. Important for understanding the regulatory environment around AI and privacy in Europe, which directly affects how ML systems process personal data.

9. [Shell Tricks That Make Life Easier (and Save Your Sanity)](https://blog.hofstede.it/shell-tricks-that-actually-make-life-easier-and-save-your-sanity/)
   - Why I recommend: Highly upvoted (473 pts) collection of shell productivity tips. Directly applicable for ML engineers who spend significant time in terminal environments running training jobs, managing datasets, and debugging pipelines.

10. [Introducing Wiz Agents & Workflows: Security at the Speed of AI](https://www.wiz.io/blog/introducing-wiz-agents)
