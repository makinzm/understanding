# Daily AI & Tech Trends - 2026-03-25

## Pick Up

1. [ARC-AGI-3](https://arcprize.org/arc-agi/3)
   - Why I recommend: The third iteration of the ARC-AGI benchmark just dropped — as an ML engineer, tracking how frontier models perform on general intelligence tasks matters for calibrating where we actually are on the capability curve.

2. [TurboQuant: Redefining AI Efficiency with Extreme Compression](https://research.google/blog/turboquant-redefining-ai-efficiency-with-extreme-compression/)
   - Why I recommend: Google Research presents a new quantization method pushing efficiency further than existing approaches; directly actionable for anyone deploying or fine-tuning large models under compute/memory constraints.

3. [Quantization from the Ground Up](https://ngrok.com/blog/quantization)
   - Why I recommend: A solid explainer on the fundamentals of quantization — pairs well with TurboQuant above. Useful for building intuition about the trade-offs before adopting any quantization library.

4. [Ensu – Ente's Local LLM App](https://ente.com/blog/ensu/)
   - Why I recommend: Local LLM tooling is maturing fast; this privacy-first application shows the direction consumer-side AI deployment is heading, which shapes data pipeline and serving decisions.

5. [Three's a Crowd: TeamPCP Trojanizes LiteLLM in Continuation of Campaign](https://www.wiz.io/blog/threes-a-crowd-teampcp-trojanizes-litellm-in-continuation-of-campaign)
   - Why I recommend: A supply chain attack targeting LiteLLM — a widely-used LLM orchestration library — is a direct threat to ML infrastructure. Essential reading if your team uses LiteLLM in production.

6. [KICS GitHub Action Compromised: TeamPCP Strikes Again in Supply Chain Attack](https://www.wiz.io/blog/teampcp-attack-kics-github-action)
   - Why I recommend: The same threat actor compromised a CI/CD security scanner. Understanding this attack chain is critical for ML teams that rely on automated pipelines for model training and deployment.

7. [Introducing the Wiz Red Agent: AI-Powered Attacker](https://www.wiz.io/blog/introducing-the-wiz-red-agent)
   - Why I recommend: Wiz introduced an AI agent that autonomously performs offensive security testing in cloud environments — a concrete example of AI agents used for security automation with real-world impact.

8. [Introducing Wiz AI Application Protection Platform (AI-APP)](https://www.wiz.io/blog/introducing-wiz-ai-app)
   - Why I recommend: A new platform category emerging around protecting AI applications (prompt injection, model poisoning, data leakage). Relevant for anyone building or operating LLM-based products in the cloud.

9. [Updates to GitHub Copilot Interaction Data Usage Policy](https://github.blog/news-insights/company-news/updates-to-github-copilot-interaction-data-usage-policy/)
   - Why I recommend: Policy changes around how Copilot handles interaction data affect teams using it for code assistance — worth reading before your organization decides its AI tooling stance.

10. [Show HN: I took back Video.js after 16 years and we rewrote it to be 88% smaller](https://videojs.org/blog/videojs-v10-beta-hello-world-again)
    - Why I recommend: A remarkable open-source stewardship story and a case study in aggressive bundle size reduction. Interesting as a lesson in software longevity and the value of revisiting legacy code.
