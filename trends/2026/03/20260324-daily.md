## Pick Up

1. [Tell HN: Litellm 1.82.7 and 1.82.8 on PyPI are compromised](https://github.com/BerriAI/litellm/issues/24512)
   - Why I recommend: Critical supply chain attack directly affecting ML engineers — LiteLLM versions 1.82.7 and 1.82.8 on PyPI were trojanized by the TeamPCP group. If you use LiteLLM as an LLM proxy/router, check your version immediately and pin to a clean release.

2. [Three's a Crowd: TeamPCP trojanizes LiteLLM in Continuation of Campaign](https://www.wiz.io/blog/threes-a-crowd-teampcp-trojanizes-litellm-in-continuation-of-campaign)
   - Why I recommend: Wiz's detailed threat intelligence report on the same LiteLLM attack — explains how TeamPCP embedded malicious code into the PyPI package and the indicators of compromise. Essential reading for anyone deploying LLM infrastructure.

3. [KICS GitHub Action Compromised: TeamPCP Strikes Again in Supply Chain Attack](https://www.wiz.io/blog/teampcp-attack-kics-github-action)
   - Why I recommend: TeamPCP also compromised the KICS GitHub Action (infrastructure-as-code security scanner) the day before the LiteLLM attack — this pattern shows an active, multi-target supply chain campaign that security-conscious ML teams should monitor.

4. [Arm AGI CPU](https://newsroom.arm.com/blog/introducing-arm-agi-cpu)
   - Why I recommend: Arm is shipping a CPU explicitly branded for AGI workloads — a hardware signal that inference and training are converging onto edge/server silicon. Worth tracking for deployment architecture decisions.

5. [Hypura – A storage-tier-aware LLM inference scheduler for Apple Silicon](https://github.com/t8/hypura)
   - Why I recommend: Addresses a real pain point for local LLM deployment — smart scheduling across DRAM/NVMe tiers on Apple Silicon. Relevant for teams prototyping on MacBooks with large models that exceed unified memory.

6. [Introducing the Wiz Red Agent - AI-Powered Attacker](https://www.wiz.io/blog/introducing-the-wiz-red-agent)
   - Why I recommend: Wiz is productizing an AI-driven red team agent — notable because it shifts offensive security from human-paced to machine-paced. Important for understanding how AI is being applied to adversarial simulation.

7. [Show HN: Gemini can now natively embed video, so I built sub-second video search](https://github.com/ssrajadh/sentrysearch)
   - Why I recommend: A concrete demo of Google Gemini's native video embeddings enabling millisecond-latency semantic video search — shows how multimodal foundation models are enabling new retrieval architectures.

8. [Missile defense is NP-complete](https://smu160.github.io/posts/missile-defense-is-np-complete/)
   - Why I recommend: A formal complexity-theoretic result with real-world implications — proves that optimal missile defense trajectory planning is NP-complete, linking classical CS theory to defense applications.

9. [Introducing Wiz AI Application Protection Platform (AI-APP)](https://www.wiz.io/blog/introducing-wiz-ai-app)
   - Why I recommend: Wiz launches a dedicated security platform for AI applications (model APIs, RAG pipelines, agents) — important signal that AI-specific attack surfaces are now large enough to warrant standalone product categories.

10. [Ripgrep is faster than grep, ag, git grep, ucg, pt, sift (2016)](https://burntsushi.net/ripgrep/)
    - Why I recommend: This 2016 benchmark post is resurfacing on HN (295 points, 130 comments) — ripgrep remains the definitive fast text search tool and the article is a great deep-dive into SIMD, DFA, and Unicode-aware matching for ML engineers who process large codebases or datasets.
